Database Systems

My First Impression of Database Systems

By Amna Khan • 10 Jun 2026 • 4 min read

Database Systems was one of the most interesting courses I studied during my second semester.

Database Learning Computer-Engineering

Why databases matter

Before taking this course, I thought databases were simply places where information was stored. However, I soon realized that they are the backbone of almost every modern application, including banking systems, hospitals, universities, and online shopping platforms. Understanding how data is stored, managed, and retrieved gave me a new perspective on technology.

During the lectures, I learned about the importance of Database Management Systems (DBMS) and how they help organize large amounts of information efficiently. This subject also introduced me to the concept of structured data and showed me why proper database design is important for improving performance and reducing errors.

Connecting theory to practice

Dr. Bilal Ahmad explained database concepts using practical examples, making it easier to understand how these ideas are applied in real-world systems. His teaching encouraged me to focus on understanding concepts rather than memorizing definitions.

Database Systems became an important part of my learning journey because it helped me understand that software development depends not only on programming but also on well-organized data management.
